Bio

I am currently a postdoctoral research associate at the Institute of Child Health, UCL undertaking a proof-of-principle study of testing translational readthrough small molecules on primary cells derived from paediatric patients with inborn errors of metabolism caused by nonsense mutations in Prof. Shamima Rahman’s research group.

Previously, I was at the University of Surrey where I initially obtained a BSc in Nutrition (2:1) with a placement year at Stockholm University in 2009/10 studying obesity and brown adipose tissue in Prof. Jan Nedergaard's group. In 2016, I completed a PhD in Nutritional Sciences and Systems Biology at Surrey, and a 3 month placement at British Nutrition Foundation, under the supervision of Dr. J. Bernadette Moore, Prof. Nick Plant, Prof. Andrzej Kierzek, and Dr. Marcus Tindall (University of Reading). My PhD investigated the use of genome scale metabolic network analyses for the study of non-alcoholic fatty liver and introduced dynamic flux variability analysis (dFVA).

Prior to joining UCL, I undertook two postdoctoral research projects with Prof. John McVey at Surrey to characterise a recombinant human factor VIII (rhFVIII) from conditioned media (Profactor Pharma) and to produce and transduce the expression of rhFVIII variants in CHO cells by lentiviral vectors (Elasmogen).
